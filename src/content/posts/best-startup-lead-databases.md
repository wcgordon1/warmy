## Deep dive on competitors

### Apollo

Apollo is the default choice for a lot of startups because it tries to do everything:

- database  
- outbound sequencing  
- enrichment  
- CRM-lite features  

It is affordable and flexible. That is the upside.

The downside is subtle but important. Apollo encourages volume.

When you have access to millions of contacts, the natural tendency is to send more instead of targeting better. That leads to:

- lower reply rates  
- more spam complaints  
- worse deliverability over time  

Apollo works best when you are disciplined. Most teams are not.

---

### ZoomInfo

ZoomInfo is built for enterprise sales teams.

Strengths:
- very deep company data  
- org charts  
- intent signals  
- strong integrations  

If you are selling large deals with long cycles, this is useful.

But for startup sales, it is often overkill.

Problems:
- expensive contracts  
- slower workflows  
- not optimized for speed  

If your strategy is “reach startups right after funding,” ZoomInfo is not built for that.

---

### Cognism

Cognism is strong in:
- EU markets  
- compliance  
- phone data  

If your team relies heavily on calling, it is worth considering.

However:
- pricing is high  
- onboarding is heavier  
- not focused on funding triggers  

It solves a different problem.

---

### UpLead

UpLead is straightforward.

- strong email verification  
- simple pricing  
- easy to use  

It is good if your main issue is bounce rate.

But it lacks:
- timing signals  
- deeper context  
- trigger-based prospecting  

It improves quality, but not relevance.

---

### Lusha

Lusha is fast and convenient.

- great for LinkedIn workflows  
- quick contact lookups  
- easy adoption  

But it is not a system for building structured outbound campaigns.

Issues:
- credit usage adds up quickly  
- not built for list building at scale  
- no real timing advantage  

It is a tool, not a strategy.
